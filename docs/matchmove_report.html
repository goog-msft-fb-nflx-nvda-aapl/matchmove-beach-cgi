01

Goal & Scene Plan

The brief was to insert multiple CGI objects into a handheld beach pan shot at Enoshima — a location with sand, ocean horizon, island structures, and ambient crowd movement. The shot is a continuous pan from left to right, lasting just under 28 seconds.

The inserted story layers a surreal moment onto the otherwise ordinary beach scene: a Hindenburg-style airship drifts across the sky, a handwritten letter flutters through the frame, and a solitary seated woman appears on the sand — all while the daytime beach light slowly warms into a golden sunset.

A surreal beach moment — deliberate, quiet, strange — designed to feel plausible before it is questioned.

02

Source Footage & Constraints

The source clip was shot handheld at Enoshima beach in portrait orientation. The camera performs a mostly horizontal pan with a small downward drift. Despite the casual handheld nature, the sand mid-ground provides a reliably textured tracking surface.

Resolution 1080 × 1920 px (portrait)
Frame rate 30 fps
Frame count 826 frames
Duration 27.5 seconds
Horiz. pan +31 px / 10-frame step (median)
Vert. drift −4.75 px / 10-frame step (median)

Key tracking challenges: sky and water regions offer no stable features; moving people are tracked as false positives; wave motion creates spurious optical flow. The stable sand mid-ground between frames 300–540 (10–18 s) was the primary anchor zone for CGI placement.

03

Video Understanding & Scene Decomposition

The source clip was described using a video-understanding model to produce a structured semantic summary. This guided subsequent decisions about tracking regions, CGI placement zones, and areas to exclude.

Sandy beach Ocean horizon Island/harbor Crowd movement Horizontal pan Handheld Sky — no track Water — no track

The model confirmed good CGI placement potential on the sand and sky, and flagged sky, water, waves, and moving people as unreliable tracking regions. The location was confirmed as Enoshima, Fujisawa, Kanagawa, Japan, which informed environmental context used in depth and segmentation passes.

04

Tracking & Camera Motion

OpenCV feature detection and optical-flow tracking were applied to sampled frames to characterize camera motion. A robust affine estimate linked each consecutive pair, with RANSAC filtering to reject moving-object outliers. The motion was classified as a rightward horizontal pan.

Analysis step 10 frames (~0.333 s)
Analyzed steps 82 steps
Median tracked pts 367.5 per step
Median RANSAC inliers 248.5 per step

05

Semantic Segmentation & Depth Context

GPU-accelerated semantic segmentation (SegFormer) produced per-frame class masks grouped into: sky, water, sand/ground, people, structures, rail/fence, and rocks. A relative depth model produced per-frame disparity maps for spatial context.

Together these two passes allowed the pipeline to identify stable sand regions, avoid people and water, and choose sensible z-depth references for the woman and airship placement in 2.5D space.

06

RANSAC Anchor Tracking for Stable Placement

Simply parenting the seated woman to the screen produces an artificial sliding look during the pan. To counter this, a single sand anchor point was manually chosen in the stable beach section and propagated through neighboring frames using RANSAC-filtered affine transforms. The resulting 2D world-locked path was used to keyframe the woman's image plane in Blender.

Tracked window 300–540 frames (10 – 18 s)
Anchor frame 447 frame index
Links / tracks 80 / 81 propagation steps
Median inliers ~1185 per RANSAC step

07

Blender Scene Construction & Object Design

The Blender scene was built as a 2.5D transparent CGI layer aligned to the source video frame dimensions. Transparent film rendering (PNG RGBA) was used throughout so the layer could be composited over the original MP4 without a background colour clash.

🛸
Hindenburg Airship

Procedural mesh: elongated hull, gondola, fins, propeller detail. Left-to-right flythrough with time-varying scale for far-to-near parallax. Slight rotation wobble prevents camera-lock artefacts.

✉️
Flying Letter

Procedural thin envelope with animated flap. Drifting, falling path with combined translation and rotation. Exits visible frame cleanly before being hidden — no sudden pop at the end.

🧍‍♀️
Seated Woman

Realistic cutout image plane (seated_woman_cutout.png). Keyed to the RANSAC sand-anchor track. No artificial black shadow — it read as fake on the detailed sand texture.

08

Compositing, Music & Sunset Color Grade

The final composite was assembled with FFmpeg in a single pass: the original video forms the base layer, the 826-frame RGBA PNG sequence from Blender is overlaid, and the background music is looped, trimmed, and faded out over the final 1.5 seconds.

Changing only Blender's world light would have warmed the CGI objects but left the real beach footage untouched — an obvious mismatch. Instead a post-process warm/orange blend is applied as a time-varying overlay to the entire composite. This makes the real beach, sky, water, and CGI elements all warm up together, creating a coherent sunset progression from normal daylight through to a golden-orange final frame.

Grade start Normal daytime light
Grade mid Warm slight amber shift
Grade end Sunset strong yellow/orange
Music fade −1.5 s from end

09

Final Result & Limitations

The final composite preserves exactly 826 frames at 30 fps. All target checks passed: same frame count as source, no letter pop at exit, no black shadow artefact under the woman, no scaling discontinuity, and a clean final frame after all CGI has left the shot.

Honest limitations of this production:

  • Full SfM reconstruction was attempted (pycolmap, 45 sampled images) but only 17 registered with 11 sparse 3D points — insufficient to drive a complete camera solve. Sky, water, sand, and moving people limited stable textured geometry across the whole pan.
  • The final camera solve is a pragmatic 2.5D matchmove driven by RANSAC-filtered 2D tracking, not a full 3D camera reconstruction. This is appropriate for the footage and invisible to the viewer.
  • The seated woman is a realistic image-plane insert, not a fully rigged or animated character. The decision not to force an interaction with the flying letter avoids unnatural contact animation.

These constraints are offset by the high inlier count of the RANSAC anchor (median ~1185), semantic masks that guided placement away from unstable regions, procedural Blender objects with motion, correctly looped music, and a full-composite sunset grade that ties all elements together.

Pipeline

Processing Steps

00
Media Extraction

Probed source video and music. Extracted metadata, sampled 12 representative frames, created source contact sheet. Output: context/media_context.json

01
Video Understanding

Described the source clip with a video-understanding model. Identified semantic regions, camera motion type, and good/bad tracking areas. Output: context/context_brief.md

02
OpenCV Feature Tracking

Analyzed camera motion with optical-flow tracking and RANSAC-filtered affine estimates. Produced track overlay video and context/camera_motion.json.

03
GPU Semantic & Depth Extraction

SegFormer semantic segmentation + relative depth estimation on the GPU server. Identified sky, water, sand, people, structures, rail, rocks. Output: masks, overlays, depth maps.

04
SfM Attempt (pycolmap)

Sparse reconstruction attempted from 45 sampled images. 17 registered, 11 3D points. Used as context; not strong enough for full camera solve due to scene characteristics.

05
RANSAC Anchor Tracking

Manually anchored a sand patch at frame 447. Propagated through frames 300–540 with RANSAC affine. Exported anchor path for Blender keyframes.

06
Blender Scene Construction

Built procedural airship, letter, and woman image plane in a 2.5D scene aligned to source frame. Transparent RGBA PNG sequence rendered: 826 frames.

07
FFmpeg Compositing

Composited CGI layer over original video. Overlaid looped+faded MP3. Applied time-varying warm sunset grade to the full composite. Exported final 826-frame result.
